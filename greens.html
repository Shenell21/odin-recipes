Southern Greens

A bit of delicious greenery to brighten up your Thanksgiving meal!

This recipe is full of flavor and will have everyone, even the kids, begging for more! Compliments and praises will flow as your loved ones fill up on this southern favorite. And the best part about this recipe? It works with ham hocks, smoked turkey wings, smoked neckbones, or any smoked meat of your choice!

Ingredients

  • 1 large bunch collard greens
  • 1 tablespoon seasoned salt
  • 1 tablespoon wet chicken base
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce of your choice
  • 1/2 lb smoked meat
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring 3 quarts of water to a boil and add smoked meat, seasoned salt, and hot sauce. Reduce heat to medium and cook for 1 hour
  2. Wash the collard greens thoroughly. Remove the stems that run down the center by holding the leaf in your left hand and stripping the leaf down with your right hand.
  3. Stack 6 to 8 leaves on top of one another, roll them up, and slice into 1/2 to 1-inch thick slices.
  4. Place greens in pot with meat and cook for 45 to 60 minutes, stirrinng occasionally.
  5. When done, taste and adjust seasoning then add butter.
